Our Mission

BoardSeatMeet's mission is to advance gender diversity within corporate boardrooms - private, public, or not-for-profit organizations - by orchestrating the social capital between board members and women who are board-ready today, or need the training to get there.

Modernizing the Approach

BoardSeatMeet Inc is a nonprofit research organization primarily organized to conduct applied research to meet a recognized need. That need is to get more women on corporate boards. We will accomplish this with a modern technology platform that will address Women Leadership Training, Management and Technical Assistance and Vocational Counseling.


Our 1:1 network orchestration platform enables seamless and personal connections between executive candidates who want to accelerate their professional trajectory and start preparing for a board seat and C level opportunities.

For High Performance

○ 76% of companies have acknowledged a business case for gender diversity in top-level leadership—but only 13% have taken the next step of calculating the positive impact.Organizations with executive-level gender diversity outperform those with homogenous executive teams by 21%.


○ Ethnic and cultural diversity also correlates with profitability—they are 33% more likely to outperform, and more successful attracting top talent, show greater employee satisfaction, and improve decision-making.


○ For gender, the executive team shows the strongest correlation of positive impact and this is where BSM can make all the difference!

For Compliance

CA Senate Bill 826 requires publicly traded companies in CA to have at least one woman on their board by the end of 2019, and half their board be women by the end of 2021 for boards with 6 or more seats.


More significantly, 29 USC Chapter 27 requires setting up a "local computerized data base referral system to maintain a current list of tradeswomen who are available for work." Since the business community did not prepare and invest in such a system, we at BoardSeatMeet are undertaking the challenge to develop a web, iOS and Android application solution to address workplace issues related to gender.
